 A silicon qubit as the quantum-era cousin of the transistor. Instead of storing information with electrical charge (“0” or “1”), it uses the tiny magnetic orientation—called spin—of a single electron that’s trapped inside a nanoscale pocket (a “quantum dot”) etched in ordinary silicon. By applying precise voltages to metal gates, engineers can load an electron into the dot, flip its spin with microwave pulses, and even make two neighboring spins interact so they perform logic together. Because all of this is built with the same CMOS fabrication tools used for today’s computer chips, silicon spin qubits promise much smaller devices, lower power, and a clear path to scaling millions of qubits on a wafer—just as the classical semiconductor industry has scaled billions of transistors.


1. **Diraq**
Sydney-born spin-out from UNSW, fabricating quantum-dot spin qubits in mainstream CMOS foundries to fast-track a million-qubit processor. [Diraq](https://diraq.com/)

2. **Quantum Motion**

London startup building dense MOS silicon qubit arrays and cryo-CMOS control—aiming for fault-tolerant architectures on the same wafers used for classical chips.  [Quantum Motion](https://quantummotion.tech/)

3. **Silicon Quantum Computing**

Led by Nobel-level researcher Michelle Simmons, SQC patterns single phosphorus donors with atomic precision to create ultra-coherent spin qubits in silicon. [Silicon Quantum Computing](https://www.sqc.com.au/)

4. **Equal 1 labs**

Integrates spin qubits and cryogenic control logic on a single CMOS die, shipping rack-mount “Q-SoC” prototypes for early application testing. [Equal 1 labs](https://www.equal1.com/)

5. **SemiQon**

Finnish fab-light venture targeting low-cost, energy-efficient spin-qubit chips to scale quantum data centres sustainably. [SemiQon](https://www.semiqon.tech/)

6. **ARQUE systems**

German spin-off from Jülich/RWTH Aachen developing a “qubit-shuttling” architecture that literally moves electron spins around the chip for long-range coupling. [ARQUE systems](https://www.arque.systems/)

7. ***Intel Quantum**

Uses its transistor know-how to mass-produce Tunnel Falls, a 12-qubit silicon spin chip now in the hands of university partners worldwide. [Intel Quantum](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/research/quantum-computing.html)

8. **HRL Laboratories**

Demonstrated universal logic with encoded three-spin qubits in Si/SiGe quantum dots, pushing fidelity toward error-correctable thresholds. 

9. **Infineon Technologies Quantum group**

R&D hub in Dresden exploring industrial-scale manufacturing of Si/SiGe spin qubits to feed Europe’s quantum supply chain. 

10. **XeedQ**

[XeedQ](https://xeedq.com/) has built a quantum processor named XQ1 which is world's first multi-qubit mobile processor. XeedQ mainly works on diamond spin based quantum systems.
