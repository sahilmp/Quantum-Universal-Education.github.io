Topology is the mathematical study of features that remain unchanged when an object is smoothly deformed—shifted, bent, or stretched—without tearing or re-attaching pieces. Topological quantum computing applies this idea by encoding information in global properties of exotic quasiparticles called anyons that emerge in certain low-temperature materials. When two anyons are exchanged, the quantum state acquires a transformation: if every pairwise exchange simply multiplies the state by a fixed phase (so the order of exchanges does not matter), the anyons are called Abelian; if different exchange sequences lead to distinct quantum states (meaning the operations do not commute), they are non-Abelian. Non-Abelian anyons are especially valuable because the resulting “braids” of world-lines can implement robust quantum logic gates whose resilience to local disturbances offers built-in error protection for future quantum computers.




**Applications of Topological quantum computing**

1. Materials discovery
2. Large-scale optimization
3. Quantum secure-cryptography.

**List of companies working in topological qubits**

1. **Microsoft Azure Quantum**

Building Majorana 1, the first silicon–superconductor “topoconductor” chip. Each qubit is encoded in four Majorana zero-modes, giving built-in error protection. Road-mapped to a million-qubit, fault-tolerant prototype under DARPA’s US2QC program. [Microsoft Azure Quantum](https://quantum.microsoft.com/) 


2. **Nokia labs**

They are pursuing non-Abelian anyon qubits formed in ultra-clean 2-D electron liquids (GaAs/AlGaAs). Their “quest for a quality qubit” programme has already shown long-lived topological states and single-charge manipulation—milestones toward a braiding-based processor. [Nokia Labs](https://www.nokia.com/bell-labs/research/air-lab/data-and-devices/topological-quantum-computing/) 

3. **Google Quantum AI**

Google Quantum AI used its superconducting-qubit platform to simulate and braid non-Abelian anyons, demonstrating topological operations on chip-scale grids—an R&D path toward hardware-level topological qubits. [Google Quantum AI](https://www.nature.com/) 

4. **Quantinuum**

Quantinuum demonstrated the first creation and manipulation of non-Abelian anyons[Quantinuum](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.03766)
